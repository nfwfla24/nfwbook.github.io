Infrastructure Is Becoming The Story

Artificial intelligence, energy systems, digital assets, and capital markets are often discussed separately.

In reality, they are becoming increasingly interconnected. AI requires power. Digital assets require settlement rails. Tokenized markets require infrastructure.

This month’s briefing examines where those systems are beginning to converge.

Energy: The Power Race Is Back

For decades, energy demand growth was slow and predictable. AI changed that.

Data centers are now competing with cities for electricity, forcing utilities, regulators, and governments to rethink how power is built. Nuclear and natural gas are emerging as the primary beneficiaries because they can provide reliable 24/7 generation at scale.

The result is a global buildout of physical infrastructure that may define the next decade. Hyperscale data centers now demand always-on, high-density electricity that renewables alone cannot reliably deliver. Tech companies are signing massive power purchase agreements, while utilities and developers accelerate new reactor builds and restarts.

Continuous Money Is No Longer A Theory

For more than a century, payroll has operated in batches. Employees work for two weeks. Companies process payroll. Funds settle days later.

Zebec is challenging that legacy model. Through streaming payments, wages can now move continuously rather than waiting for scheduled pay periods.

The model gained another major step forward through Zebec’s partnership with Stellar, enabling global stablecoin payroll infrastructure designed for real-world businesses.

As of May 2026, approximately 65,000 workers were already receiving compensation through Zebec-powered systems.

Why Regulatory Clarity Matters

For years, digital asset companies have operated under overlapping and often conflicting regulatory frameworks.

The CLARITY Act seeks to establish clearer boundaries between securities, commodities, and payment assets.

Clear rules rarely create excitement, but they often create the foundation required for long-term adoption.

Infrastructure scales faster when participants understand the rules.

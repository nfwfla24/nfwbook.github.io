Featured Intelligence

Energy: AI's New Bottleneck

Artificial intelligence is often described as a software revolution. In reality, it is rapidly becoming an infrastructure story. Every new generation of AI models requires increasingly powerful data centers operating around the clock. Those facilities demand reliable electricity, cooling systems, transmission capacity, and long-term energy security.

For decades, computing advances were largely constrained by chips, software, and networking. Today, power availability is emerging as an equally important limiting factor. A shortage of generation capacity can delay entire data center projects regardless of how advanced the technology inside them may be.

AI may be digital, but its constraints are increasingly physical.

That reality is driving renewed investment in nuclear energy, natural gas generation, grid modernization, transmission infrastructure, and next-generation reactor technology. Major technology firms are increasingly exploring direct energy partnerships because reliable electricity is becoming a strategic asset rather than simply an operating expense.

As AI adoption accelerates, the race for compute may ultimately become a race for power. Investors focused solely on software may be missing the infrastructure layer that makes the entire system possible.

The companies enabling power generation, transmission, cooling, and data center construction may play a critical role in the next phase of AI expansion. Infrastructure is becoming the story.

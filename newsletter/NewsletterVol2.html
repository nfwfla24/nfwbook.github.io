Crypto: CLARITY Act Faces Critical June Deadline

The CLARITY Act cleared the Senate Banking Committee on May 14, 2026, in a 15-9 vote with two Democratic Senators supporting. But the clock is ticking: the Senate has only 18 working weeks left in 2026, and the bill hasn't completed its first remaining step with the Agriculture Committee [web:31][web:42][web:43].

⚠️ Why This Deadline Matters: 5 Critical Reasons

1. Regulatory Uncertainty Costs Billions: Since Bitcoin's inception, the SEC and CFTC have jostled for control over crypto regulation. This "bureaucratic tug-of-war" has created enforcement chaos, with companies facing surprise actions and unable to plan. The CLARITY Act ends this [web:45].

2. Innovation Stalled Without Rules: Major crypto firms including Coinbase have lobby for years for this framework. Without clear rules, businesses can't comply safely, face >$1B in enforcement costs, and investors lack protections. The Act provides "easier compliance, less risk of surprise enforcement, and more room to innovate" [web:45][web:48].

3. 2026 Election Year Pressure: Passing by July 2026 is critical because "preferably June" is the deadline for 2026 enactment. Every week lost at Step 1 (Banking Committee) cannot be recovered at Steps 2-5. After Easter recess (March 30) and before midterm campaign recess (October 5), there are only 18 working weeks [web:31][web:42].

4. Stablecoin Yield Battle Unresolved: The central dispute—whether crypto platforms can pay yield on stablecoin holdings—remains unresolved after the White House's March 1 deadline expired without compromise. This affects billions in USDC/USDT holdings and DeFi protocols [web:42].

5. First Domino Must Fall: The late-March Banking Committee markup was "the first domino." If it doesn't fall before March 30, the next window opens April 13. Now that Banking passed it (May 14), the Agriculture Committee must act. If either step fails, the entire bill collapses before reaching the President [web:42].

The Act creates three regulatory categories that will define the industry: Digital Commodities (CFTC) including Bitcoin and Ethereum, Investment Contracts (SEC + CFTC) for ICO tokens and capital raising, and Payment Stablecoins (Banking Regulators) like USDC and USDT [web:24][web:43].

A key provision creates a "mature blockchain test" allowing tokens to move from SEC to CFTC oversight once sufficiently decentralized. DeFi developers get safe harbors exempt from securities laws if engaged solely in infrastructure activity [web:28][web:43].

"After months of negotiation, outreach and delay, the CLARITY Act finally passing out of the Senate Banking Committee is an important step toward a long-awaited US regulatory framework for digital assets."

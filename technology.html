BRIS and VRL: reliability methodologies for critical infrastructure.

The New Financial World LLC develops the Baseline Reliability Intelligence System (BRIS) and the Verified Reliability Layer (VRL) as methodologies for structuring truth and trust around energy infrastructure. Both were invented by Zakir Sadiki, and both are designed for utilities, regulators, and institutional finance teams—not as software products to buy, but as ways of organizing canonical records and public attestation.

BRIS · The Layer of Truth

BRIS — Baseline Reliability Intelligence System

BRIS is the private, canonical record of asset health inside an investor‑owned utility (IOU). It is designed for internal use by the utility, capturing the full asset‑health history in a way that supports operations, engineering, and regulatory reporting.

What BRIS is

A methodology for how an IOU can structure and maintain its own canonical reliability record — the “layer of truth” for asset health data that never leaves the utility boundary.

Type
Private, IOU‑internal system — the canonical record of asset health.
Who uses it
Investor‑owned utilities (IOUs), internally.
What it records
Full asset‑health history: events, states, and relevant reliability metadata.
Why it stays private
Asset‑health data is sensitive and operationally critical. BRIS keeps this record inside the IOU’s boundary while still enabling attestation via VRL.
Reference implementation
Hyperledger Fabric — a permissioned, enterprise‑grade distributed ledger framework.
VRL · The Trust Layer

VRL — Verified Reliability Layer

VRL is the public anchor for reliability attestation. It never publishes the asset data itself; it publishes only a hash, timestamp, and opaque reference, creating a chain‑agnostic trust layer that other parties can verify without seeing the underlying records.

What VRL publishes

VRL publishes hash + timestamp + opaque reference only — the attestation anchor, not the record. This allows utilities to prove the existence and integrity of their BRIS records without exposing operationally sensitive data.

Type
Public anchor for reliability attestation.
Contains
Hash + timestamp + opaque reference — never the underlying asset‑health record.
Ledger posture
Chain‑agnostic. Hedera is live on testnet today, with Stellar, Energy Web, Solana, Ethereum, XRP, and Sui planned as additional ecosystems.
Grant posture
Designed to be grant‑eligible across multiple blockchain ecosystems because it functions as an open trust layer rather than a single‑chain product.

VRL phased deployment model

VRL is structured to roll out in phases, starting from a single‑utility pilot and progressing toward a member‑governed clearing house that operates the trust layer for a shared reliability namespace.

Phase 1

Single‑IOU pilot

One utility anchors its own reliability attestations into VRL, tying its internal BRIS record to a public trust anchor while keeping asset data private.

Phase 2

Multi‑IOU shared namespace

Multiple utilities begin publishing into a shared attestation namespace, establishing a common, chain‑agnostic layer of trust around reliability records.

Phase 3

Member‑owned clearing house

A member‑governed clearing house operates the trust layer, stewarding the shared namespace and the rules for reliability attestation across participating utilities.

BRIS and VRL — how they relate

BRIS and VRL are two layers of a single methodology: BRIS is the canonical record inside the IOU, and VRL is the public attestation anchor others can verify. Both were invented by Zakir Sadiki, and both are presented here as methodologies for structuring reliability — not as products.

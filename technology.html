BRIS
Methodology 01

Baseline Reliability Intelligence System

The private intelligence layer for energy asset health. BRIS is the canonical record of what happened, when, and how reliably — producing a defensible score that lives inside the utility's walls and never leaves.

Core Principles

Semantics over substrate
BRIS owns the semantics of reliability intelligence — the scoring logic, record schema, and canonical definitions. The ledger is a swappable substrate. Hyperledger Fabric is the reference implementation, not a dependency.
Tamper-evident timing
BRIS is designed to preserve evidence of when bad data was produced — the architecture must prove the timing and duration of anomalies if challenged legally or in a regulatory proceeding.
Fully IOU-internal
The full bris-record/1 schema never leaves the utility's internal network. Regulators and external parties receive only a verified hash — never raw asset intelligence or proprietary scoring parameters.
VRL integration is optional
BRIS is fully standalone. VRL is framed as a compatible output channel — not a requirement. An organization can deploy BRIS without ever publishing to a public chain.

VRL
Methodology 02

Verified Reliability Layer

The public trust layer for reliability attestation. VRL is producer-agnostic — it accepts a hash and opaque reference from any upstream evidence source and anchors it to a public chain, enabling tamper-evident verification without exposing raw data.

Core Principles

Producer-agnostic
VRL does not require BRIS as an upstream source. It accepts a hash and opaque reference from any evidence producer that conforms to the vrl-anchor/1 schema. BRIS is one example implementation.
Lean public anchor
The production target is a minimal opaque anchor: root hash, timestamp, and an opaque reference — nothing more. Self-description happens via disclosed leaves, not by publishing raw records to the chain.
Chain-agnostic by design
VRL is not limited to any single L1 attestation service. Hedera is the live testnet reference implementation. Adapter architecture supports Stellar, Energy Web, Avalanche, Solana, Ethereum, XRP Ledger, and Sui.
Regulator read access without operational control
Regulators (MRC/NERC) receive verified read access to anchored attestations. They can verify the hash and timestamp — they never gain operational control over the private BRIS data or the IOU's internal ledger.

Deployment Model

Phased operator rollout

VRL is designed for a phased adoption path — from single-IOU pilot to member-owned clearing house, with regulatory read access throughout but no operational control ever granted to external parties.

01
Single-IOU Pilot

One utility deploys VRL with full control. Your system, your control. BRIS feeds the anchor; VRL publishes to the Hedera testnet (moving to mainnet in production). Proof-of-concept for internal stakeholders.

Your system, your control
02
Multi-IOU Shared Namespace

Multiple utilities share a VRL anchor namespace with co-endorsement. Each IOU retains full control of its BRIS data. Regulators gain shared read access across the participating utilities without any operational role.

Shared anchor namespace
03
Member-Owned Clearing House

VRL spins out to a neutral, member-owned entity using a Clearing House model. NFW monetizes hosted services and operator fees. The protocol itself remains open. Regulator read access is preserved with zero operational control.

Clearing House model
